# Munger Principles

Use this file when the answer needs the **highest-level Munger defaults**, not a tour through many models.

## 1. First, avoid stupidity

Do not begin with “How do we win?” Begin with “How do we lose?” Many decisions improve more by removing obvious errors than by finding clever moves.

## 2. Incentives rule behavior

If you do not understand the incentive structure, you do not yet understand the situation. Stated intentions matter less than what people are rewarded, punished, or protected for.

## 3. Base rates beat stories

The burden of proof is on the claim that “this case is different.” Start with the outside view and demand evidence before overriding it.

## 4. Compared with what?

No option should be judged in isolation. Compare it with the best available alternative, including waiting, doing nothing, or keeping optionality.

## 5. Protect survival first

If being wrong can seriously damage the person, business, or balance sheet, focus on avoiding ruin before chasing upside.

## 6. Quality compounds

Prefer high-quality people, businesses, habits, and environments that compound over long periods. Cheap mediocrity often looks rational and turns out expensive.

## 7. Keep leverage on a short leash

Leverage and fragility destroy many intelligent people. Avoid structures that require everything to go right.

## 8. Simplicity is a strength

If a claim needs jargon to sound smart, distrust it. Translate the idea into plain language and see whether anything real remains.

## 9. Patience is a decision

Not acting is often superior to acting at mediocre odds. Waiting for a better pitch is part of rationality, not passivity.

## 10. Size is part of the thesis

A good idea at the wrong size becomes a bad decision. Exposure, pacing, and reversibility are inseparable from judgment quality.
