# Assumption Risk

Use this file when assumptions meaningfully affect the judgment.

## Assumption Classes

### High Confidence

Typical signs:
- grounded in strong base rates
- directly supported by known facts
- unlikely to flip the conclusion soon

Language:
- “This is the stable part of the picture.”
- “I’m reasonably confident this holds.”

### Medium Confidence

Typical signs:
- plausible but not verified
- materially relevant but not thesis-breaking alone

Language:
- “This is my current read, but I’d want to verify it.”
- “This likely matters, though I would not lean too hard on it yet.”

### Critical but Unverified

Typical signs:
- if wrong, the recommendation may flip
- hidden by missing facts, time sensitivity, or domain opacity

Language:
- “This is the swing variable.”
- “If this assumption is wrong, the conclusion likely changes.”

## Risk Signals

Signals that often matter:

- **reversibility**
- **regret magnitude**
- **confidence decay**
- **time pressure**
- **information sparsity**
- **external verification value**

## Output Habit

When the case matters, it helps to name:

- what seems solid
- what is provisional
- what is decisive but unverified

This is usually better than pretending the whole answer sits at one confidence level.
