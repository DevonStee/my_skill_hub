# A. Meta Models

Use this file when the main problem is **how to frame the decision**, not just which option to choose. These models help define the question, force the outside view, and keep the reasoning honest.

This is usually the **first reference file** to load after `core/munger-principles.md`. If the question is confused, under-specified, or overly story-driven, start here before touching anything more specialized.

## Munger Default Order

When in doubt, reach for these first:

1. inversion
2. circle of competence
3. base rates
4. margin of safety
5. map is not the territory

For each model, ask three things:
- **Use when** this pattern is distorting or clarifying the decision
- **Ask** the question that pressure-tests the situation
- **Watch for** the failure mode that makes smart people look foolish

## Model 1 — Latticework of Mental Models
- **Use when:** the issue spans multiple domains and one lens feels too narrow.
- **Ask:** which two or three disciplines explain this better together than alone?
- **Watch for:** using one favorite framework to explain everything.

## Model 2 — Circle of Competence
- **Use when:** confidence exceeds direct experience or domain knowledge.
- **Ask:** what do I actually know from first-hand understanding rather than borrowed confidence?
- **Watch for:** speaking with precision outside genuine competence.

## Model 3 — Inversion
- **Use when:** the upside story sounds persuasive but fragile.
- **Ask:** what would make this fail, and am I already doing any of those things?
- **Watch for:** planning from hope instead of from avoidable error.

## Model 4 — First Principles
- **Use when:** the discussion is trapped in convention, analogy, or inherited assumptions.
- **Ask:** what has to be true at the physics/economics/behavior level for this to work?
- **Watch for:** cargo-culting what worked elsewhere without the same underlying conditions.

## Model 5 — Base Rates / Outside View
- **Use when:** the case is being sold as exceptional.
- **Ask:** how do similar situations usually end before I tell myself this one is special?
- **Watch for:** inside-view storytelling with no reference class.

## Model 6 — Probabilistic Thinking
- **Use when:** the answer is uncertain but decisions still must be made.
- **Ask:** what are the likely ranges, not just the favorite narrative?
- **Watch for:** treating maybe/likely/almost certain as the same thing.

## Model 7 — Expected Value
- **Use when:** outcomes vary wildly in payoff or downside.
- **Ask:** probability multiplied by consequence points to what decision?
- **Watch for:** obsessing over vivid upside while ignoring magnitude-weighted downside.

## Model 8 — Second-Order Effects
- **Use when:** the immediate benefit looks obvious.
- **Ask:** if this works on the first move, what happens next and who adapts?
- **Watch for:** solving the first-order problem by creating a worse downstream one.

## Model 9 — Margin of Safety
- **Use when:** estimates are noisy, leverage is present, or downside matters.
- **Ask:** how wrong can I be before this becomes a bad decision?
- **Watch for:** plans that require unusually favorable assumptions.

## Model 10 — Bayesian Updating
- **Use when:** new evidence arrives after an initial view is formed.
- **Ask:** what fact would cause me to meaningfully change my mind?
- **Watch for:** collecting data as ammunition instead of as correction.

## Model 11 — Map Is Not the Territory
- **Use when:** models, metrics, decks, or narratives feel cleaner than reality.
- **Ask:** what part of the real world is this abstraction hiding?
- **Watch for:** mistaking the spreadsheet, theory, or dashboard for the thing itself.

## Quick Selection Guide

- **Question is poorly framed:** 2, 3, 4, 11
- **Question sounds overconfident:** 2, 5, 6, 10
- **Question involves large downside:** 3, 7, 8, 9
- **Question spans several domains:** 1, 5, 8, 11

## High-Frequency Bundle

For many important decisions, a solid default bundle is:

- 3 — inversion
- 5 — base rates
- 9 — margin of safety
- 11 — map is not the territory

Use this bundle when the story is seductive, the downside matters, and the user may be narrating rather than thinking.
