# B. Psychology of Misjudgment

Use this file when the decision is being bent by emotion, persuasion, status, identity, social pressure, or self-serving reasoning. These are not side issues. They are often the whole ballgame.

Munger was right to treat psychology as a primary operating system, not a decorative layer. In live decisions, these are the highest-frequency distortions. When a smart person is acting foolishly, this file usually explains why.

## Munger High-Frequency Traps

Reach here first for:

- 12 — incentive-caused bias
- 16 — inconsistency-avoidance
- 19 — envy / status pressure
- 24 — overoptimism
- 25 — deprival-superreaction
- 26 — social proof
- 36 — lollapalooza

## Model 12 — Incentive-Caused Bias
- **Use when:** behavior looks irrational but compensation or status rewards it.
- **Ask:** what outcome does this person get paid, praised, or protected for?
- **Watch for:** moralizing what is really an incentive problem.

## Model 13 — Liking / Loving Tendency
- **Use when:** admiration for a person, company, or story feels unusually strong.
- **Ask:** what defects am I discounting because I want this to be true?
- **Watch for:** affection substituting for analysis.

## Model 14 — Disliking / Hatred Tendency
- **Use when:** contempt makes it easy to dismiss a person or idea.
- **Ask:** what would I say if the same facts came from someone I respected?
- **Watch for:** rejecting good ideas because of the messenger.

## Model 15 — Doubt-Avoidance Tendency
- **Use when:** there is pressure to decide fast just to stop discomfort.
- **Ask:** am I resolving uncertainty, or merely escaping it?
- **Watch for:** false clarity purchased by premature closure.

## Model 16 — Inconsistency-Avoidance Tendency
- **Use when:** someone clings to an old view because changing it is embarrassing.
- **Ask:** if I had no prior public commitment, would I still defend this?
- **Watch for:** defending yesterday’s decision with today’s reasoning.

## Model 17 — Curiosity Tendency
- **Use when:** the situation rewards investigation instead of opinion.
- **Ask:** what is the most decision-relevant fact I still have not checked?
- **Watch for:** pseudo-curiosity that gathers trivia but avoids decisive evidence.

## Model 18 — Kantian Fairness Tendency
- **Use when:** norms and reciprocity shape cooperation.
- **Ask:** what behavior would people view as fair enough to sustain trust?
- **Watch for:** exploiting people in a way that invites retaliation or withdrawal.

## Model 19 — Envy / Jealousy Tendency
- **Use when:** the urge to keep up is stronger than the case itself.
- **Ask:** would I still want this if nobody else could see it?
- **Watch for:** status competition disguised as strategic thinking.

## Model 20 — Reciprocation Tendency
- **Use when:** favors, gifts, or kindness create hidden obligation.
- **Ask:** am I rewarding generosity, or being steered by it?
- **Watch for:** feeling compelled to say yes because something was given first.

## Model 21 — Mere Association Tendency
- **Use when:** a decision benefits from halo effects, prestige, or vibes.
- **Ask:** what part of the appeal comes from association rather than substance?
- **Watch for:** borrowing credibility from brands, titles, or aesthetics.

## Model 22 — Pain-Avoiding Psychological Denial
- **Use when:** the obvious conclusion is emotionally costly.
- **Ask:** what truth would hurt enough that I would prefer not to see it?
- **Watch for:** analytical sophistication used to avoid basic reality.

## Model 23 — Excessive Self-Regard Tendency
- **Use when:** the person sees themselves as above average in difficult domains.
- **Ask:** what evidence says I am not the exception?
- **Watch for:** self-image outrunning results.

## Model 24 — Overoptimism Tendency
- **Use when:** plans assume favorable execution, timing, and luck simultaneously.
- **Ask:** what assumptions are all leaning in the same direction?
- **Watch for:** “best case” quietly becoming the default case.

## Model 25 — Deprival-Superreaction Tendency
- **Use when:** losing an option feels unbearable.
- **Ask:** am I defending value, or reacting to perceived loss?
- **Watch for:** paying too much because the alternative feels like surrender.

## Model 26 — Social-Proof Tendency
- **Use when:** popularity is being mistaken for correctness.
- **Ask:** if the crowd disappeared, what would remain of the case?
- **Watch for:** consensus as substitute for evidence.

## Model 27 — Contrast-Misreaction Tendency
- **Use when:** an option looks good only relative to something worse.
- **Ask:** would this still look attractive if assessed on a blank page?
- **Watch for:** anchoring on a bad comparison set.

## Model 28 — Stress-Influence Tendency
- **Use when:** fatigue, fear, urgency, or overload narrow thinking.
- **Ask:** is the current emotional state reducing decision quality?
- **Watch for:** treating stress reactions as strategic insight.

## Model 29 — Availability-Misweighing Tendency
- **Use when:** vivid recent examples dominate judgment.
- **Ask:** what important evidence is absent because it is not memorable?
- **Watch for:** recent stories overpowering representative data.

## Model 30 — Use-It-or-Lose-It Tendency
- **Use when:** neglected capabilities, relationships, or disciplines are decaying.
- **Ask:** what useful habit or edge am I letting atrophy?
- **Watch for:** assuming dormant strengths stay available without upkeep.

## Model 31 — Drug-Misinfluence Tendency
- **Use when:** substances materially affect judgment or behavior.
- **Ask:** is chemistry distorting perceived confidence, risk, or self-control?
- **Watch for:** treating altered judgment as authentic clarity.

## Model 32 — Senescence-Misinfluence Tendency
- **Use when:** aging or cognitive decline may affect accuracy or adaptability.
- **Ask:** where might reduced stamina or flexibility matter here?
- **Watch for:** pretending time does not change capabilities.

## Model 33 — Authority-Misinfluence Tendency
- **Use when:** hierarchy or prestige pushes people toward compliance.
- **Ask:** what would this look like if the same claim came from a peer?
- **Watch for:** obedience replacing independent judgment.

## Model 34 — Twaddle Tendency
- **Use when:** jargon is being used to create the appearance of depth.
- **Ask:** can this claim survive translation into plain language?
- **Watch for:** decorative complexity with no operational meaning.

## Model 35 — Reason-Respecting Tendency
- **Use when:** people need a rationale to cooperate or accept friction.
- **Ask:** what explanation would feel legitimate to the people affected?
- **Watch for:** issuing instructions without a reason and then acting surprised by resistance.

## Model 36 — Lollapalooza Tendency
- **Use when:** multiple biases and incentives all point the same way.
- **Ask:** which three or more forces are reinforcing the same bad move?
- **Watch for:** intelligent people making extreme mistakes because everything in the environment pushes one direction.

## Quick Selection Guide

- **Validation-seeking user:** 16, 22, 23, 24
- **Crowd-driven decision:** 19, 25, 26, 29, 36
- **Persuasion / manipulation risk:** 12, 20, 21, 33, 35
- **Emotionally loaded call:** 15, 22, 28, 36

## Use This File Especially When

- the user already sounds committed and wants permission
- the story is emotionally hot but evidentially thin
- a crowd, authority figure, prestige signal, or scarcity cue is doing too much work
- intentions sound noble but the incentive structure points elsewhere
