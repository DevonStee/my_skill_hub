# D. Science, Systems, and Organizations

Use this file when the decision depends on system behavior over time: compounding, fragility, reliability, adaptation, bottlenecks, thresholds, or coordination. These models help explain why apparently small forces produce very large outcomes.

This is the file for questions where time and system structure matter more than clever opinion. It is especially important for understanding **compounding, fragility, ruin, bottlenecks, and why subtraction often beats addition**.

## Munger Default Systems Questions

Ask these early:

- what compounds here, for good or ill?
- what is fragile?
- where is the bottleneck?
- what should be removed before anything new is added?
- what breaks if the system is stressed?

## Model 64 — Compounding
- **Use when:** repeated small gains or losses accumulate.
- **Ask:** what happens if this repeats for years instead of weeks?
- **Watch for:** focusing on one-period effects in a compounding system.

## Model 65 — Regression to the Mean
- **Use when:** recent performance is unusually extreme.
- **Ask:** how much of this is likely to normalize without any intervention?
- **Watch for:** treating temporary extremes as permanent quality.

## Model 66 — Fat Tails and Power Laws
- **Use when:** a few outcomes may dominate the distribution.
- **Ask:** is this a world where rare events matter more than averages?
- **Watch for:** using normal-case intuition in highly skewed domains.

## Model 67 — Nonlinearity
- **Use when:** small input changes can create disproportionate results.
- **Ask:** where does this system stop behaving in a straight line?
- **Watch for:** linear forecasting across nonlinear thresholds.

## Model 68 — Exponential Growth and Decay
- **Use when:** growth, decline, debt, or contagion can accelerate.
- **Ask:** what doubles, halves, or snowballs here?
- **Watch for:** underestimating acceleration because early stages look tame.

## Model 69 — Feedback Loops
- **Use when:** outputs feed back into future behavior.
- **Ask:** what reinforcing or balancing loop dominates this system?
- **Watch for:** fixing symptoms while strengthening the loop causing them.

## Model 70 — Bottlenecks
- **Use when:** one narrow constraint limits the whole system.
- **Ask:** what is the single slowest, rarest, or most fragile link?
- **Watch for:** optimizing everything except the actual constraint.

## Model 71 — Throughput / Theory of Constraints
- **Use when:** capacity, workflow, or execution speed matters.
- **Ask:** what change would increase the system’s total output rather than local efficiency?
- **Watch for:** improving busywork while throughput stays flat.

## Model 72 — Critical Path
- **Use when:** sequential dependencies determine timing.
- **Ask:** which step truly governs the completion date?
- **Watch for:** celebrating parallel progress that does not move the deadline.

## Model 73 — Redundancy
- **Use when:** failure is costly and backup matters.
- **Ask:** where do I need duplicate capacity instead of lean elegance?
- **Watch for:** designing single points of failure into important systems.

## Model 74 — Slack and Buffers
- **Use when:** shocks, delay, or variability are inevitable.
- **Ask:** what reserve of time, cash, talent, or inventory keeps this from becoming brittle?
- **Watch for:** running so lean that ordinary volatility becomes crisis.

## Model 75 — Reliability and Failure Chains
- **Use when:** one defect can cascade through the whole system.
- **Ask:** if one part breaks, what breaks next?
- **Watch for:** isolated-risk thinking in tightly coupled systems.

## Model 76 — Engineering Tolerance / Error Bounds
- **Use when:** measurement, prediction, or execution errors are unavoidable.
- **Ask:** what level of deviation can this system safely tolerate?
- **Watch for:** plans that require precision the real world will not provide.

## Model 77 — Entropy and Maintenance Load
- **Use when:** complexity grows unless actively managed.
- **Ask:** what ongoing upkeep does this choice create?
- **Watch for:** choosing elegant launches that become ugly maintenance burdens.

## Model 78 — Adaptation and Selection
- **Use when:** competition or environment filters out weak designs over time.
- **Ask:** what traits does this environment reward or punish?
- **Watch for:** admiring traits that are impressive but not fitness-relevant.

## Model 79 — Fitness Landscapes and Local Maxima
- **Use when:** the current solution is decent but may trap future improvement.
- **Ask:** am I optimizing inside a local hill while missing a better hill?
- **Watch for:** small optimizations that lock in a bad architecture.

## Model 80 — Emergence
- **Use when:** group behavior cannot be explained by one component alone.
- **Ask:** what pattern appears only at the system level?
- **Watch for:** blaming individuals for behavior produced by structure.

## Model 81 — Path Dependence
- **Use when:** history limits today’s options.
- **Ask:** what past choices are constraining present flexibility?
- **Watch for:** assuming a clean-slate option set in a legacy system.

## Model 82 — Irreversibility
- **Use when:** a decision is costly or impossible to unwind.
- **Ask:** what door closes permanently if I do this?
- **Watch for:** treating one-way doors like two-way doors.

## Model 83 — Thresholds, Critical Mass, and Tipping Points
- **Use when:** systems behave differently once a threshold is crossed.
- **Ask:** what minimum level of adoption, trust, density, or pressure changes the game?
- **Watch for:** linear effort applied below the level where effects begin.

## Model 84 — Scale Effects
- **Use when:** size changes quality, coordination, or economics.
- **Ask:** what works at 10 units but breaks at 10,000?
- **Watch for:** extrapolating small-scale success to large-scale operation.

## Model 85 — Coordination Costs
- **Use when:** many people, teams, or interfaces must align.
- **Ask:** how much friction does each added handoff create?
- **Watch for:** adding people and meetings as if coordination were free.

## Model 86 — Via Negativa
- **Use when:** removing failure sources may matter more than adding brilliance.
- **Ask:** what should be stopped, avoided, or simplified first?
- **Watch for:** adding features and policies to problems caused by excess.

## Model 87 — Goodhart’s Law
- **Use when:** metrics, targets, or KPIs drive behavior.
- **Ask:** what breaks once the measure becomes the target?
- **Watch for:** optimizing the score while damaging the underlying reality.

## Quick Selection Guide

- **Operational fragility:** 70, 73, 74, 75, 76
- **Growth over time:** 64, 68, 69, 83, 84
- **Complex organizations:** 72, 77, 80, 81, 85, 87
- **Decision reversibility:** 65, 67, 78, 79, 82, 86

## High-Frequency Bundle

For many hard real-world decisions, a powerful default bundle is:

- 64 — compounding
- 70 — bottlenecks
- 74 — slack and buffers
- 82 — irreversibility
- 86 — via negativa

Use this bundle when the issue is not just “what should we do?” but “what keeps this from becoming fragile, dumb, or permanently expensive?”
