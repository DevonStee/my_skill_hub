# Decision Archetype Hints

This file is not a template and not a workflow. It is a reminder of what different kinds of decisions are **really about** when viewed through a Munger-shaped lens.

Use it only when an archetype helps you see the decision more clearly. Ignore it when it adds noise.

## Binary Choice

**What this is really about:**
- what objective actually matters
- what the real alternative is, including doing nothing
- which downside is irreversible

**What people often miss:**
- they compare the exciting option to a strawman, not to the real status quo

## Opportunity Evaluation

**What this is really about:**
- whether the opportunity is good or merely exciting
- what must be true for it to work
- whether it is better than the best available alternative

**What people often miss:**
- they confuse narrative attractiveness with quality and odds

## Failure Diagnosis

**What this is really about:**
- what changed
- what the actual bottleneck is
- what should be stopped before anything new is added

**What people often miss:**
- they treat symptoms as causes and answer failure with more activity

## People Decision

**What this is really about:**
- what incentives shape the person’s behavior
- whether this is a quality, integrity, or boundary problem
- what happens if the judgment is wrong

**What people often miss:**
- they let likability, charisma, or status outrun evidence

## Capital Allocation

**What this is really about:**
- whether the size fits the uncertainty
- how much downside can be survived
- how much optionality remains if the thesis is only partly right

**What people often miss:**
- they evaluate the idea but not the exposure

## Commitment / Exit

**What this is really about:**
- whether this is still a good bet from today forward
- how much staying is driven by sunk cost or identity
- what becomes possible if time, capital, or attention are freed up

**What people often miss:**
- they defend the past instead of reallocating from the present

## General Reminder

Archetypes are hints, not boxes. Mixed cases are normal.
